Intranet

Knowledge & Intranet

The current SharePoint intranet rebuilt and modernized inside Flight Deck. Links and tools, the marketing and brand asset library, and HR reference material, all in one place so the team stops toggling between platforms.

01 / Overview

The brief

The intranet today is functional but was built outside the skill set it needed. Content is duplicated, and what looks like around twenty sections actually nests into a handful of real domains. It carries links and tools (JetHQ links, industry tools, listing platforms), a team directory, time-zone reference, a marketing and brand asset library, and HR reference material. Flight Deck rebuilds it modern and clean so the team never returns to the old intranet.

The goal is one world, not two. If a section is not covered in Flight Deck, people still have to go back, so the migration maps every domain first before anything is rebuilt. Aircraft listings and files are handled in the separate Hub and Aircraft Files workstream and are not duplicated here.

02 / Objectives

What success looks like

  • Links and tools rebuilt as clean, navigable sections: JetHQ links, industry tools, and listing platforms.
  • A team directory with contact info and roles, plus time-zone reference the team uses every day.
  • A searchable marketing and brand asset library with direct links to Canva templates.
  • The JetHQ brand identity and templates easy to find, so anyone builds on-brand without hunting.
  • HR reference material available as quick reference, at hand when the team needs it.
  • Sensitive HR and compensation files kept out of Flight Deck, stored on OneDrive with leadership and legal only.
  • One home for reference, so no one returns to the old intranet.

03 / Scope

Scope of work

Stage 1 · Around Aug 2026

Map the domains, rebuild links and directory

The team reaches every link, tool, and colleague from one clean place, with nothing left behind on the old intranet.

  • Map every existing intranet domain first, so the rebuild covers all of it and nothing forces a trip back.
  • Rebuild links and tools as clean, navigable sections: JetHQ links, industry tools, and listing platforms.
  • Build the team directory with contact info and roles.
  • Add the time-zone reference the team relies on across regions.

Stage 2 · Around Sep to Oct 2026

Marketing and brand asset library

The team finds brand assets and templates in seconds and builds on-brand without leaving Flight Deck.

  • Stand up the searchable marketing and brand asset library.
  • Link directly to Canva templates so the right template is one click away.
  • Publish the JetHQ brand identity kit so it is easy to find and use.
  • Carry over the SOP and marketing folders Johnny has already started so that work is not lost.

Stage 3 · Around Oct to Nov 2026

HR reference and the final cutover

Everyday HR reference is at hand, sensitive files stay protected, and the old intranet is retired for good.

  • Publish HR reference material as quick reference: the employee handbook, the foundation book, and policies.
  • Keep sensitive HR and compensation files on OneDrive with leadership and legal, never in Flight Deck.
  • Optional interactive org chart, noted as low priority and built only if JetHQ wants it.
  • Confirm every domain is covered so the old intranet can be retired.
