Project OS

Delivery Tracking

JetHQ's version of Project OS. Valuation and fair market value requests logged and counted, sales-engineer workload visible at a glance, and a live pulse on who is active in which region. It replaces the manual counting the team does in Monday today.

01 / Overview

The brief

The team tracks work in Monday today, but uses a single layer, essentially a checklist to count things: valuation and FMV requests, market refreshes, and prospecting. Requests often arrive by group chat, and a sales engineer enters them manually so they can be counted. Leadership wants to see how much the sales engineers are being asked for and who is active by region.

Delivery Tracking gives JetHQ a proper version of this: requests logged in one place, workload visible per engineer, and a regional pulse. It sits as a workspace inside Flight Deck and can be a pinned shortcut for each sales engineer. Project OS can do far more than this. The scope is set to what JetHQ needs today, with room to grow.

02 / Objectives

What success looks like

  • Log and count valuation and FMV requests, tagged by requester, region, and aircraft type.
  • Show sales-engineer workload at a glance, such as requests per day per engineer.
  • Give leadership a regional activity pulse showing who is active and where.
  • Move requests in-platform, so a rep requests an FMV in Flight Deck instead of by chat.
  • Deliver a single view leadership can read without asking someone to pull a report.
  • Live as a workspace inside Flight Deck, pinnable per person.
  • Leave room to grow into fuller project tracking later.

03 / Scope

Scope of work

Stage 1 · Around Nov 2026

Request and task tracking

The team logs and counts requests exactly the way it uses Monday today, now inside Flight Deck.

  • A single-layer checklist that mirrors how the team works in Monday now.
  • Valuation and FMV requests logged in one place.
  • Market refreshes and prospecting captured alongside requests.
  • A running count of each request type, no manual tally required.

Stage 2 · Around Dec 2026

Dashboards and in-platform requests

Leadership reads workload and regional activity at a glance, and reps request an FMV without leaving Flight Deck.

  • A workload dashboard showing requests per day per engineer.
  • A regional dashboard showing who is active and where.
  • Requester, region, and aircraft-type tagging on every request.
  • An in-platform FMV request form that feeds the tracker directly.

Stage 3 · Around Jan 2027

Optional growth into project tracking

If JetHQ wants more depth later, the workspace grows into fuller project tracking.

  • Optional subtasks under a request or project.
  • Optional threaded comments for coordination.
  • Scoped and sized only if JetHQ needs it after the first two stages are live.
