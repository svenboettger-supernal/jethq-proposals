CRM

CRM

JetHQ's own CRM and the long-term replacement for Salesforce. Every deal in one pipeline, emails captured automatically, and each aircraft linked to its client so a name in the Hub opens the client record.

01 / Overview

The brief

This is the larger effort in the plan and the one that moves JetHQ off Salesforce over time. Today the team pays for Salesforce, pays for add-ons to make it fit, and spends engineering and RevOps time bandaging it. On the entry Salesforce tier, the built-in AI captures only the primary account holder's emails and no text messages, so a deal worked by two reps loses half its history. A native CRM inside Flight Deck captures emails across everyone on a deal, holds deals in one pipeline, and links each aircraft to its client.

It is the third circle of the Venn: the CRM, the Hub, and the aircraft data source all meet inside Flight Deck. Sales adoption is the KPI that unlocks real return, and a CRM that lives inside the platform the team already opens removes the friction of a separate login. This is a considered direction. Supernal partners with RevOps to get there, and the near-term Salesforce work continues in parallel.

02 / Objectives

What success looks like

  • One pipeline for every deal, from lead to close.
  • Automatic email capture across every rep on a deal, not just the primary contact.
  • Each aircraft linked to its client, so a name in the Hub opens the client record and an aircraft opens its owner.
  • The CRM as the source of truth for deals, wired into Flight Deck and the Hub.
  • The manual Salesforce effort and the stack of add-on subscriptions removed over time.
  • A surface the sales team actually adopts because it is where they already work.
  • A clean migration path from Salesforce that keeps history.

03 / Scope

Scope of work

Stage 1 · Around Jan to Feb 2027

The data model and the first records

Contacts, deals, and aircraft-to-client links land, and new web-form leads flow straight in.

  • The contacts and deals model, built for how JetHQ actually works a deal.
  • Aircraft-to-client linking, so each aircraft resolves to its client and back.
  • Capture of inbound web-form leads directly into the CRM.

Stage 2 · Around Feb to Mar 2027

Email capture and the working pipeline

The whole deal team's email is logged automatically and deals move through visible stages.

  • Automatic email capture and logging across the whole deal team, not just the primary contact.
  • Pipeline stages that reflect how a JetHQ deal progresses.
  • Deal and aircraft views that cross-reference, so one opens the other.

Stage 3 · Around Mar to Apr 2027

The transition off Salesforce

The CRM becomes the source of truth for deals and the add-ons come off the bill, history intact.

  • Migrate off Salesforce as the source of truth, on a schedule that keeps the team working throughout.
  • Retire the add-on subscriptions once the CRM carries the load.
  • Optional text-message capture, if JetHQ wants messages logged alongside email.
