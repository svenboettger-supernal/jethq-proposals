Proposal for JetHQ

Our partnership

JetHQ Flight Deck

One company operating system, structured exactly as the prototype lays it out. A Workspace the whole team lives in, from listings and SOPs to sales and finance, and an Intelligence layer that answers questions across all of it. The Hub keeps its name and stays the single source for listings; Flight Deck links to it and brings everything else into one place.

Executive Summary

Today the team moves between the Hub, the SharePoint intranet, Salesforce, Monday, Microsoft 365, Canva, a WhatsApp channel for scheduling, and shared drives to get through a day. Flight Deck removes the toggling. It mirrors the modules the team already sees in the prototype, grouped into a Workspace and an Intelligence layer, and builds them in the order the team needs. The low-effort wins come first: the home screen, the SOP library, people, and onboarding. The heavier Sales Ops and Admin and Finance work follows once the foundation is live. Two principles hold throughout. The Hub stays the single source for listings, so nothing is duplicated. And every module is a real surface the team opens, not a report someone has to pull.

Workspace

The eleven surfaces the team lives in

The Workspace is where the whole team works every day. Each module below maps one to one to a tile in the prototype sidebar, in the same order. Phase tags mirror the prototype and set a rough build order, not fixed dates.

01 · Phase 2

Mission Control

The customizable home screen. Each person lands on the KPIs, shortcuts, and widgets that match their role: who is working, the deals they have done, the listings that are active. Web and mobile from day one.

02 · Live

Markets & Listings

The Hub, where listings already live. Flight Deck links straight to it and back, so listings and market notes stay in one place with no duplication. Aircraft files consolidate onto each aircraft record.

03 · Phase 2

SOP Library

Every SOP pulled over from Microsoft 365 into one searchable library. A summer intern is lined up to help with the migration.

04 · Phase 2

People Hub

A living org chart and team directory: who is on which team, their role, and their tenure with the company. Modeled on a directory Supernal has built before.

05 · Phase 2

Onboarding

The onboarding and general biz-ops flows currently stuck in Monday, rebuilt where the team will actually use them. A low-effort, high-value early win.

06 · Phase 3

Marketing & Creative

The brand book, templates, and creative assets unified from Microsoft 365 and Canva into one place the marketing team can work from.

07 · Phase 3

Tools & Platforms

A vendor and license portal: every tool the company pays for, with renewal dates, a link to the contract, and a link to the platform. For the solutions engineers and internal IT.

08 · Phase 3

Calendars

The primary company calendar, replacing the Microsoft calendar no one uses and the priorities traded back and forth over WhatsApp.

09 · Phase 4

Sales Ops

The Yearly Results made real-time. Salesforce connected by API and visualized: inbound leads, pipeline, deal health, comp, and leaderboards, with listings sourced only from the Hub so nothing is duplicated.

10 · Phase 4

Admin & Finance

Accounts payable and receivable and the finance operations that today run on the most expensive headcount. The largest workstream in the plan, scoped with Supernal.

11 · Phase 5

Company Timeline

A live feed of what is happening across the company, from new listings to closed deals, so the team has a shared sense of momentum.

Intelligence

An answer layer across everything

The Intelligence layer sits on top of the Workspace and answers questions across all of it.

12 · Phase 5

Ask JetHQ

The company second brain. Ask a question in plain language and get a sourced answer from across Flight Deck: listings, SOPs, people, deals, and documents. This is where Marcus lives.
