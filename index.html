Our partnership

JetHQ Flight Deck

One operating system for the whole team. Flight Deck is the place everyone opens first: a role-based home screen, the modernized intranet, delivery tracking, and the CRM, all wired to the Hub so the team stops toggling between platforms and works from a single source of truth.

Executive Summary

Today the JetHQ team moves between the Hub, the SharePoint intranet, Salesforce, Monday, and shared drives to get through a day. Every jump is friction, and every platform is one more place to check. Flight Deck removes the toggling. It is a single web and mobile operating system where each person lands on a home screen built for their role, reaches every tool and document from one place, and drops into the Hub for a listing without leaving the surface. The Hub stays the source of truth for aircraft and keeps its name; Flight Deck surfaces it, links to it, and adds the intranet, delivery tracking, and CRM around it. The build is phased so the low-effort, high-value pieces ship first and the heavier CRM work follows once the foundation is live.

The operating system

Three surfaces the team lives in

Flight Deck opens on a home screen, holds the intranet the team references all day, and links straight into the Hub. These three ship first because they carry the most people and the least build risk. The Hub keeps its name and its job; Flight Deck wraps around it.

Home

Flight Deck Home

A role-based heads-up display. Active listings, deals against goal, Hub quick access, calendar, and live activity, arranged per person with customizable pin shortcuts. The KPIs a broker sees differ from what leadership sees. Web and mobile from day one.

Intranet

Knowledge & Intranet

The current SharePoint intranet rebuilt and modernized inside Flight Deck: JetHQ links, industry tools, and listing platforms; the searchable marketing and brand asset library with Canva links; and HR reference material. Sensitive HR and compensation files stay on OneDrive with leadership.

Aircraft

Hub & Aircraft Files

Flight Deck surfaces every listing and deep-links to the aircraft page in the Hub. Aircraft files, FMV, maintenance records, marketing, photos, specs, and the pitch deck, are consolidated onto each aircraft so brokers pull the full story from one place. New-listing routing keeps the Hub as the source.

Running the business

Delivery and deals in one place

Once the foundation is live, Flight Deck takes on how work is requested and tracked and how deals are managed. Project OS lands first as a natural extension of what the team already does in Monday. The CRM is the larger effort and follows, built to earn the team off Salesforce over time.

Project OS

Delivery Tracking

JetHQ's version of Project OS. Valuation and FMV requests logged and counted, sales-engineer workload visible at a glance, and a live pulse on who is active in which region. Replaces the manual counting the team does in Monday today, with room to grow into full project tracking.

CRM

CRM

JetHQ's own CRM and the long-term replacement for Salesforce. Every deal in one pipeline, emails captured automatically, and each aircraft linked to its client so a name in the Hub opens the client record. Built to remove the manual Salesforce effort and the stack of add-on subscriptions.

On the platform

An assistant, when it earns its place

An optional in-platform helper for the everyday language work the team does, kept separate from the core build so it never blocks it.

Assistant

AI Helper

A simple in-platform assistant for translation, grammar, and drafting, so the team stops leaving Flight Deck for ChatGPT. An optional private-model path keeps sensitive legal work local and can replace a per-matter legal tool like GCAI. Scoped and sized only if JetHQ wants it.
