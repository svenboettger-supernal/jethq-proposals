Package 02

Markets & Listings

The Hub stays the source of truth for aircraft and keeps its name. Markets and Listings is the tile that clicks straight out to the Hub and back, so listings live in one place with no duplication, and the aircraft files are consolidated onto each aircraft so the whole story lives in one place.

01 / Overview

The brief

Aircraft files today are scattered across shared drives, one folder per aircraft, holding the fair market value, maintenance records, marketing and the exclusive marketing agreement, photos, videos, specifications, and the pitch deck. Brokers hunt across folders to pull a file for a client, and the story of an aircraft is spread across places nobody wants to open twice.

Flight Deck surfaces the Hub listings by state and links to the aircraft page in the Hub, where the notes and market updates already live. The files move onto the aircraft record so a broker opens one page and has everything. The Hub remains the source of truth: new aircraft still enter the Hub, and Flight Deck reflects them.

02 / Objectives

What success looks like

  • Every listing surfaced in Flight Deck with a deep link to its aircraft page in the Hub.
  • Every listing state represented: active with agreements, acquisitions, off-market, coming soon, offers, pending, sold, and non-JetHQ history.
  • Aircraft files consolidated onto the aircraft record: fair market value, maintenance records, marketing and the marketing agreement, photos, videos, specifications, and the pitch deck.
  • A broker opens any file for a client from one place, with no hunting across folders.
  • New-listing routing so an aircraft entering the Hub also appears in Flight Deck automatically.
  • Status toggles such as mark as coming soon or mark as a new listing, controlled by the operator.
  • Mobile access so brokers pull files with a client in the room.

03 / Scope

Scope of work

Stage 1 · Q3 2026

Surface listings and deep-link to the Hub

Every listing shows up in Flight Deck by state and opens straight to its aircraft page in the Hub.

  • Read the Hub listings and surface them in Flight Deck grouped by state.
  • Represent each listing state: active with agreements, acquisitions, off-market, coming soon, offers, pending, and sold.
  • Deep-link each listing to its aircraft page in the Hub, where notes and market updates already live.
  • Confirm the Hub stays the source of truth and Flight Deck reflects what the Hub holds.

Stage 2 · Q3 2026

Consolidate aircraft files onto the record

Every file for an aircraft lives on that aircraft, so a broker opens one page and has the whole story.

  • Consolidate fair market value, maintenance records, marketing and the marketing agreement, photos, videos, specifications, and the pitch deck onto each aircraft record.
  • Migrate the current shared-drive folders per the chosen approach: files moved fully in, or linked in place.
  • Retain non-JetHQ listing history for reference alongside the JetHQ record.
  • Let a broker open any file for a client from the aircraft page.

Stage 3 · Q3 2026

Routing, toggles, and mobile access

New aircraft flow in automatically, the operator controls status, and brokers reach files from their phone.

  • New-listing routing so an aircraft entering the Hub also appears in Flight Deck without manual entry.
  • Status toggles such as mark as coming soon or mark as a new listing.
  • Mobile access to files so brokers pull the fair market value or a video with a client in the room.
  • Operator handover so listings and files are administered day to day inside JetHQ.
